## The Origin Story: December 2020

### The Project Background

In Fall 2020, Wake Forest University's Master of Science in Management (MSM) program partnered with **E. & J. Gallo Winery** for a semester-long **Graduate Consulting Project (GCP)**. Sponsored by Gallo's Winston & Salem Houses (Sales and Marketing departments), the project challenged student teams to address a critical business question:

> **"With the increase in spiked seltzer trends, consumers (mainly Millennials and Generation Z) are drinking less wine and/or spirits. How should Gallo handle this shift?"**

Gallo had recently launched **Barefoot Seltzer**, the **only wine-based seltzer product currently in the market**, and sought actionable insights on two key challenges:

1. **Competitive Positioning:** How can Barefoot Seltzer compete with category leaders like White Claw and Truly in attracting Millennial/Gen Z consumers?
2. **Channel Strategy:** How can Gallo capitalize on the growth in the convenience channel to maximize Barefoot Seltzer sales?

Student teams conducted comprehensive market research, consumer analysis, and sales data evaluation to propose strategies for expanding Gallo's market share and strengthening its presence in the rapidly growing seltzer category.

The competition concluded in **December 2020** with final presentations, during which student teams delivered their recommendations‚Äîincluding both incremental changes Gallo could immediately implement and innovative solutions aimed at capturing the next generation of beverage consumers.

## Our Team's Approach

### Why Focus on China?

Our team (**Group 25**) chose a bold, differentiated strategy: instead of competing directly with White Claw and Truly in the saturated North American market, we proposed **entering the Chinese market** as a first-mover opportunity.

**The North American Context (2020):**
- Hard seltzers were exploding: growing at 200-300% annually
- Market size: ~$4-5 billion and rapidly expanding
- White Claw dominated with ~60% market share
- Truly held ~25% share
- New entrants (Bud Light Seltzer, Bon & Viv, Corona) flooding in
- Competition was fierce and capital-intensive

**The China Opportunity (2020):**
- Emerging hard seltzer market with minimal competition
- Massive consumer base: 400+ million Millennials and Gen Z
- Growing interest in Western lifestyle and drinking culture
- E-commerce infrastructure enabling rapid product launches
- Local competitors (RIO, Horoyoi) focused on different positioning
- **First-mover advantage** potential

Our hypothesis: By entering China early with a premium, wine-based positioning, Barefoot could establish brand leadership before the market became crowded‚Äîavoiding the fierce North American competition.

## The Research: 273 Consumer Surveys
**[View Surveys](DATA_DICTIONARY.md)**

### Survey Design & Execution

We designed a comprehensive consumer survey to understand Chinese hard seltzer preferences, purchasing behavior, and brand perception.

**Survey Specifications:**
- **Sample Size:** 273 responses
- **Target Demographic:** Chinese consumers, ages 21-35
- **Geographic Focus:** Tier 1 & 2 cities (Shanghai, Beijing, Shenzhen, Guangzhou, Hangzhou)
- **Platform:** Qualtrics (distributed via WeChat, social media, university networks)
- **Timeline:** 3 weeks (October-November 2020)
- **Response Rate:** ~65% completion rate

**Survey Structure:**

1. **Demographics (8 questions)**
   - Age, gender, income level, city, education
   - Drinking frequency and alcohol preferences

2. **Hard Seltzer Awareness & Trial (6 questions)**
   - Brand recognition (White Claw, Truly, RIO, Horoyoi)
   - Previous trial experience
   - Occasions for consumption

3. **Product Preferences (12 questions)**
   - Flavor preferences (citrus, tropical, berry, local flavors)
   - Packaging preferences (glass vs. can, size, design)
   - ABV preferences (4%, 5%, 6%+)
   - Sweetness level

4. **Purchase Behavior (10 questions)**
   - Preferred channels (e-commerce, retail, bars, convenience stores)
   - Price sensitivity and willingness to pay
   - Frequency and occasion-based purchasing
   - Influencers of purchase decisions (KOL, friends, ads)

5. **Brand Positioning (8 questions)**
   - Perception of wine-based vs. malt-based seltzers
   - Health and wellness associations
   - Premium vs. mass-market positioning
   - Brand attribute preferences

## Analytical Frameworks Applied

We employed multiple strategic frameworks to structure our analysis:

### 1. STEEPG Analysis
Evaluated macro-environmental factors affecting the Chinese hard seltzer market:
- **Social:** Growing health consciousness, Western lifestyle trends, female drinking culture
- **Technology:** E-commerce dominance, mobile payment infrastructure, KOL marketing
- **Economics:** Rising middle class, discretionary spending growth
- **Environment:** Sustainability concerns (packaging, waste)
- **Policy:** Alcohol regulations, import duties, advertising restrictions
- **Global:** Hard seltzer trends from US, international brand expansion

### 2. SWOT Analysis for Barefoot in China

**Strengths:**
- Established Gallo wine brand equity
- Wine-based unique positioning
- International brand credibility
- Production and distribution capabilities

**Weaknesses:**
- Limited brand awareness in China
- No existing distribution network
- Higher import costs vs. local competitors
- Cultural unfamiliarity with "seltzer" category

**Opportunities:**
- First-mover advantage in premium segment
- E-commerce rapid launch capability
- Health & wellness consumer trend
- Female target market underserved

**Threats:**
- Local competitors with lower costs (RIO)
- Cultural preference for sweeter drinks
- Regulatory complexity
- Rapid market saturation potential

### 3. Competitive Benchmarking

| Brand | Base | Market Position | Price Point | Target |
|-------|------|----------------|-------------|---------|
| **White Claw** | Malt | US market leader | Premium | Young professionals |
| **Truly** | Malt | US #2 brand | Mid-premium | Millennials |
| **RIO** | Malt | China local leader | Mass market | Young females |
| **Horoyoi** | Shochu | Japan import | Premium | Urban sophisticates |
| **Barefoot** | Wine | Proposed entrant | Premium | Health-conscious females |

### 4. Brand Positioning Framework

Applied **Keller's Brand Equity Pyramid** to map Barefoot's potential positioning:
- **Salience:** Wine-based, health-conscious, premium
- **Performance:** Low calorie, natural ingredients, sophisticated taste
- **Imagery:** Western lifestyle, female empowerment, social elegance
- **Judgments:** Quality, credibility, superiority
- **Feelings:** Confidence, social approval, wellness
- **Resonance:** Brand loyalty, community engagement

## Our Strategic Recommendations

Based on 273 survey responses and strategic analysis, we proposed a comprehensive market entry strategy:

### Target Market

**Primary Segment:**
- Urban Chinese females, 25-35 years old
- College-educated, middle to upper-middle income
- Health-conscious, seeking low-calorie alternatives
- Active on social media, influenced by KOLs
- Western lifestyle adopters (travel, fashion, fitness)

**Secondary Segment:**
- Young urban males, 28-35
- Professionals in tier 1 cities
- Social drinkers seeking variety

### Positioning Strategy

**Core Positioning:**  
*"Premium wine-based hard seltzer for the health-conscious, sophisticated Chinese consumer"*

**Key Differentiators:**
- **Wine-based** (vs. malt-based competitors)
- **Health & Wellness** (low calorie, natural)
- **Premium Quality** (Gallo wine heritage)
- **Western Sophistication** (aspirational lifestyle)

**Tagline Concept:**

### Marketing Mix (4 Ps)

**Product:**
- 330ml glass bottles (primary)
- 355ml cans (convenience channel)
- Flavors: Grapefruit, Mango, Peach, Lychee (China exclusive)
- 5% ABV (balanced for Chinese preference)
- "Wine-based" prominently featured on label

**Price:**
- Premium positioning: **¬•20-25 per unit** (~$3-4 USD)
- 15-20% above local competitors (RIO ~¬•15-18)
- Justified by wine heritage and quality ingredients

**Place (Distribution):**
- **Phase 1:** E-commerce (Tmall flagship store, JD.com)
- **Phase 2:** Specialty beverage stores (Ole, City Super)
- **Phase 3:** Premium bars and restaurants
- **Phase 4:** Convenience stores (7-Eleven, FamilyMart)

**Promotion:**
- KOL partnerships (lifestyle, fitness, travel influencers)
- WeChat/Xiaohongshu content marketing
- Sampling campaigns at fitness studios and events
- Co-branding with healthy lifestyle brands

## Key Learnings

**Consumer Research:**
- 273 responses provided statistically meaningful insights
- Cultural context matters: Chinese consumers valued e-commerce (68%) and premium packaging differently than North American markets
- Balanced quantitative and qualitative questions captured depth

**Strategic Analysis:**
- Frameworks (STEEPG, SWOT) guided comprehensive thinking
- Every recommendation tied back to survey data, not assumptions
- Clear KPIs and phased approach demonstrated realistic planning

**Data Visualization:**
- Design for multiple audiences: executives need insights quickly, analysts want drill-down details
- Use annotations to guide viewers to key findings
- Less decoration, more clarity and storytelling
