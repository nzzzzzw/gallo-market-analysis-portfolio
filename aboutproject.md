## The Origin Story: December 2020


In Fall 2020, Wake Forest University's Master of Science in Management (MSM) program partnered with **E. & J. Gallo Winery** for a semester-long **Graduate Consulting Project (GCP)**. 

**The Challenge:**  
> **"With the increase in spiked seltzer trends, consumers (mainly Millennials and Generation Z) are drinking less wine and/or spirits. How should Gallo handle this shift?"**


**Our Focus:**  
Gallo had launched **Barefoot Seltzer** (the only wine-based seltzer) and needed insights on:
1. **Competitive Positioning:** How to compete with the category leaders
2. **Channel Strategy:** How to maximize sales in the vartity channels

Student teams presented in December 2020, proposing both **actionable strategies** for immediate implementation and **innovative concepts** to capture the next generation of consumers.

## Our Team's Approach

### Why Focus on China?

Our team (**Group 25**) chose a differentiated strategy: instead of competing in the saturated North American market, we proposed **entering China as a first-mover**.

**North American Context (2020):**
- Hard seltzers growing 200-300% annually
- Market size: ~$4-5 billion
- White Claw: ~60% share | Truly: ~25% share
- Fierce competition with new entrants flooding in

**China Opportunity (2020):**
- Emerging market with minimal competition
- 400+ million Millennials and Gen Z consumers
- Strong e-commerce infrastructure
- Local brands (RIO, Horoyoi) differently positioned
- **First-mover advantage potential**

**Our Research Question:**

>**Can premium wine-based positioning enable Barefoot to capture early-mover advantage in China's emerging hard seltzer market?**

## The Research

### Consumer Questionnaire

We designed a comprehensive survey to understand Chinese consumer preferences, behavior, and brand perceptions.

**Survey Specifications:**
- **Sample Size:** 273 responses
- **Target:** Chinese consumers, ages 21-35
- **Location:** Tier 1 & 2 cities (Shanghai, Beijing, Shenzhen, Guangzhou)
- **Platform:** Qualtrics (via WeChat, social media, university networks)
- **Timeline:** 3 weeks (October-November 2020)

### Analytical Frameworks

We applied multiple strategic frameworks to structure our analysis and ensure comprehensive market understanding:

- **STEEPG Analysis** - Macro-environment evaluation
- **SWOT Analysis** - Barefoot's strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, threats
- **Competitive Benchmarking** - Brand positioning comparison
- **Keller's Brand Equity Pyramid** - Brand positioning framework

#### 1. STEEPG Analysis
Macro-environmental factors affecting the Chinese hard seltzer market:
- **Social:** Health consciousness, Western lifestyle trends, female drinking culture
- **Technology:** E-commerce dominance, mobile payments, KOL marketing
- **Economics:** Rising middle class, discretionary spending growth
- **Environment:** Sustainability concerns (packaging, waste)
- **Policy:** Alcohol regulations, import duties, advertising restrictions
- **Global:** Hard seltzer trends from US, international brand expansion

#### 2. SWOT Analysis for Barefoot in China

**Strengths:** Gallo brand equity, wine-based differentiation, international credibility  
**Weaknesses:** Limited awareness, no distribution network, higher import costs  
**Opportunities:** First-mover advantage, e-commerce capability, health trends  
**Threats:** Local competitors (lower costs), cultural preferences, regulatory complexity

## Our Strategic Recommendations

Based on 273 survey responses and strategic analysis, we proposed a comprehensive market entry strategy.

#### Target Market
**Primary:** Urban Chinese females, 25-35, college-educated, health-conscious, active on social media  
**Secondary:** Young urban males, 28-35, professionals seeking variety

#### Positioning
**Core Message:** *"Premium wine-based hard seltzer for the health-conscious, sophisticated Chinese consumer"*

**Key Differentiators:** Wine-based (vs. malt), health & wellness focus, premium quality, Western sophistication
